<person>Sachithra Senanayake</person>, Sri Lanka's offspinner, has been fined 30 per cent of his match fee for violating the International Cricket Council's (ICC) Code of Conduct during the first Twenty20 International (T20I) played against Australia in Pallekele on Tuesday (August 7). <person>Senanayake</person> breached Article 2.1.7 of the ICC Code of Conduct for Players and Player Support Personnel, pertaining to "using language, actions or gestures which disparage or which could provoke an aggressive reaction from a batsman upon his dismissal during an international match". After <person>David Warner</person>, the Australian captain, was castled by a tossed up delivery from <person>Senanayake</person>, the offspinner vented out his frustration in a verbal tirade. <person>Javagal Srinath</person>, the match referee, levied a fine of 30 per cent. <person>Senanayake</person> accepted the sanction in front of the match referee.
